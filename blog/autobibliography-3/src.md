## Early influences

- **Lenore M. Edman and Windell H. Oskay of [Evil Mad Scientist](https://www.evilmadscientist.com)** ([RSS feed](https://www.evilmadscientist.com/feed/))

    The first folks whose maker blog I started following. Lots of cool stuff around building pen plotters, and their [Interactive LED Coffee Table](https://www.evilmadscientist.com/2007/interactive-led-coffee-tables/)---as demonstrated by Harley Cat---was a particularly formative project that I've thought about regularly over the years:

- **Amy, aka sailorhg, of [electrocuties](https://electrocuties.com/) and [sailorhg](https://sailorhg.com)** [<img class="link" alt="Instagram" src="./assets/instagram-logo.png"/>](https://www.instagram.com/sailorhg/)

    Aesthetically pleasing projects and zines. I think I first encountered sailorhg through [this functional transit card ring](https://electrocuties.com/2019/11/29/one-ring-to-ride-them-all-electrocuties) made by dissolving an RFID-based transit card. I got as far as the "dissolving the card" part; I used a cheapo plastic keychain for mine, instead of going the resin route, and it was very satisfying to use to tap onto the T.[^card]

    [^card]: Unfortunately I stalled so long on making mine that my city's transit system enabled their tap-to-pay system basically within a month of me dissolving my card, and it was easier to switch over to using my phone. Ah well. ðŸ™ƒ
